Debate Motion

Young people can preserve local culture better through digital media than through traditional community events.

Choose Your Position

Select one position before preparing your rebuttal.

Opponent’s Argument

Some people argue that traditional community events are still the best way to preserve local culture because they allow young people to experience cultural values directly, not only through screens.

Rebuttal Structure

Use this structure to respond politely and clearly:

  • Acknowledge: I understand the point that ...
  • Disagree or qualify: However, I believe that ...
  • Reason: This is because ...
  • Evidence or example: For example, ...
  • Closing: Therefore, ...

Moderator Instruction

Welcome to Unit 3. Please respond to the opponent’s argument. Your task is to give a clear rebuttal while staying polite and logical.

  • Refer briefly to the opponent’s point.
  • Use polite expressions such as “I understand your point, but...”
  • Give one clear reason to support your rebuttal.
  • You may use examples from social media, digital storytelling, local festivals, cultural performances, or youth communities in Kepulauan Riau.
  • Speak for approximately two minutes.

Prepare Your Rebuttal

Write short notes before speaking. Focus on responding to the opposing view. Do not write a full script.

Self-Check and Future AI Feedback

Later, this section can be connected to AI-driven feedback. For now, students can use the checklist below after speaking.

  • Did I respond to the opponent’s argument?
  • Did I use polite rebuttal language?
  • Did I give a clear reason for my rebuttal?
  • Did I use examples related to local culture or digital media?
  • Did I avoid attacking the speaker personally?
  • Did I speak clearly and confidently?
Future AI feedback may include clarity, fluency, grammar, vocabulary, pronunciation, rebuttal structure, politeness, and relevance to local culture.
